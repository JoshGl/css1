Table of Hostas

Cultivar name Parentage Frequency in trade Description What makes it special?
Hosta venusta 'Ki Nakafu Otome' Sport of H. venusta. Relatively rare. This sport of Hosta venusta features a special variegation: emerging leaves display a lime-green center of each leaf. By the end of the season, the lime-green centers fade into the same deep green as the margins. 'Ki Nakafu Otome' is a Japanese selection of an already rare Hosta species, known in the wild only from middle elevations of Jeju Island in South Korea. This shape-shifting variegated cultivar is just as vigorous as the species, and makes a lovely rock garden specimen or kusamono feature.
Hosta x 'Fire Island' H. longipes hypoglauca x 'Crested Surf' Quite common. This is one of the finest of the golden Hostas, standing the test of time ever since its introduction onto the market by Bill Brincka in 1998. Ovate lemony leaves ripple above speckled red stems. Great color upon emerging, really nice form, vigorous growth. Just all around a lovely choice for container or garden.
